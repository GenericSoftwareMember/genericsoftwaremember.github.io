The Tower

The sixteen card, the Tower, is a peculiar one. The reason being is that its meaning, sudden upheaval, chaos, and revelation, are often negative. This means the Tower is likely the only card that has little to no positive interpretations compared to the rest, and is seen as an omen to get, even if upright. Due to this, some decks have actually removed the Tower entirely.


Upright

When The Tower appears upright in a reading, it signifies a profound and unforeseen change or revelation that shakes the foundations of your life. This card represents the need to break free from old structures, beliefs, or situations that no longer serve your growth. While the experience may be unsettling, it is often necessary for personal transformation and rebuilding. The Tower encourages you to embrace change as an opportunity for growth and renewal.


Reversed

In a reversed position, The Tower may indicate a resistance to change or a delayed crisis. You might be avoiding addressing necessary upheaval or clinging to unstable situations. This card advises you to confront issues head-on and not postpone the inevitable. Avoiding change may lead to a more catastrophic event later. Embrace the idea of dismantling what no longer serves you and rebuilding your life with a stronger foundation.
