The Hierophant

The Hierophant, the fifth card in the Major Arcana, symbolizes tradition, spirituality, and guidance. The card actually has numerous different names depending on the set. It is sometimes called the Pope or the High Priest.


Upright

When The Hierophant appears upright in a reading, it signifies a time of seeking guidance from established traditions or spiritual sources. It represents a need for structure, faith, and conformity to societal norms. This card encourages you to turn to mentors, teachers, or spiritual advisors for wisdom and support. It suggests a commitment to a spiritual or moral path and can indicate important ceremonies or rites.


Reversed

In a reversed position, The Hierophant may suggest a resistance to tradition or a desire to break free from societal or religious norms. You might be questioning established beliefs and seeking a more individualistic path. This card encourages you to explore your spirituality and ethics independently, but also warns against rebelling without thoughtful consideration. It could also indicate a need to find unconventional sources of guidance or a period of uncertainty regarding your beliefs.
