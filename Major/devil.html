The Devil

The Devil, the fifteenth card in the Major Arcana, represents temptation, bondage, and materialism. It often depicts a horned figure with chains around the necks of a man and woman, symbolizing their entrapment by their desires. The devil in question, at least in the Rider-Waite deck used for all of these cards, is Baphomet, a demon supposedly worshipped by the Knight Templars during the Crusades. The evidence that the Christian knights did such a thing are minimal and mostly came around centuries after.


Upright

When The Devil appears upright in a reading, it signifies a sense of entrapment, materialism, or unhealthy attachments. This card warns against succumbing to temptation and becoming enslaved by worldly desires. It encourages you to examine your life and identify areas where you may be giving in to unhealthy habits or negative influences. Breaking free from these chains requires self-awareness and the courage to face your inner demons.


Reversed

In a reversed position, The Devil may indicate a release from bondage or a break free from unhealthy attachments. You might be overcoming temptations or letting go of negative influences. This card signifies liberation from oppressive situations and encourages you to regain control over your life. It can also represent a period of self-discovery and healing as you free yourself from what once held you back.
