The Hermit

The Hermit, the ninth card in the Major Arcana, represents introspection, solitude, and inner guidance. It often features a cloaked figure holding a lantern, symbolizing the search for inner wisdom.


Upright

When The Hermit appears upright in a reading, it signifies a period of introspection, solitude, and inner searching. This card represents a time for self-reflection, seeking answers within, and connecting with your inner wisdom. The Hermit encourages you to withdraw from external distractions and noise to find clarity, understanding, and enlightenment. It's a reminder that answers to your questions can be found within, and this introspective journey can lead to profound insights.


Reversed

In a reversed position, The Hermit may indicate a reluctance to seek solitude or a fear of introspection. You might be avoiding self-reflection or resisting the need for inner guidance. This card encourages you to embrace moments of solitude and self-discovery. It may also suggest that you are seeking answers outside of yourself rather than looking within. Reconnect with your inner wisdom, and trust in your ability to find the answers you seek.
