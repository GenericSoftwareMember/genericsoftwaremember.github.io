What are Tarot Cards?

Tarot cards are a deck of seventy-eight cards that were created around the 1400s. Originally, they were just normal playing cards for regular card games before they were believed to have further meaning and began to be used in cartomancy, or fortune‑telling and divination using cards. When used for this purpose, they are referred to as "Arcana", and are separated into two different categories: Major or Minor.


Major

The Major Arcana are comprised of twenty‑two different cards, with each one possessing a different meaning. When it comes to tarot card reading, it follows a principle known as the "Fool's Journey", in which the cards have a chronological order and represent the path people travel down in life. They use Roman numerals and number I to XXI, with the titular Fool being either unnumbered or treated as 0 or XXII. The naming of each Arcana and the number associated with each one may differ slightly between decks, but listed below are one of the more common modern interpretations that are typically used in divinations.

Minor

The Minor Arcana are comprised of the remaining fifty-six cards, separated into four suits. There are fourteen cards in each suit, with ten numbered cards going from 1-10 and the last remaining four court cards being a king, queen, knight, and jack. The four suits were originally the batons, coins, cups, and swords, but their names have been altered in the modern day. Nowadays, the batons suit is usually substituted with the wands or staves, the coins is often renamed to the pentacles, and the cups can also be called goblets.

Upright and Reversed

Depending on how the card is drawn from a tarot deck, the meaning of the card can change. If a card is drawn in the upright position, it generally ascribes the positive attributes associated with the card. If, however, the card is drawn upside-down, or reversed as it is known in tarot readings, that means that the opposite meanings are given. It can also mean that a challenge relating to that card's meaning will be present soon, or highlight the area in life where you are struggling.
